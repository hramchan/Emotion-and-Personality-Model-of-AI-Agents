Some appraisal mechanism is needed for elicitation of emotions, to accomplish such task, involves tools such as “fuzzy logic” for evaluating an intelligent agent, in this case, “God”. Fuzzy computation for mentioned intelligent agent is used because there is no precise value to assign how other agents appraise the events with relation to “God” and evaluating emotion of “love”.

   Fuzzy rules are used to determine the intensity of generated emotions in terms of fuzzy values. (Jain et al 2015) Reading further of fuzzy logic there are five basic appraisal variables includes the following:

     Emotions var (Evar) =  Desirability (D), Expectedness (E), OutcomeProbability (OP), Suddenness (S), and CauseHarm (CH).

D = {UD, N, LowD, MediumD, HighD}    

      Variable: Desirable can be evaluated as undesirable, low desirable, medium desirable, high desirable                                                                                            

E = {UE, N, LowE, MediumE, HighE}

      Variable: Event can be evaluated as  unexpected, low expected, medium expected, high expected  

S = {low, medium, high}

      Variable: Suddeness can be evaluated as low, medium, or high.

OP = {VUP, UP, N, P, VP}

      Variable: OutcomeProbability can be evaluated as very unpleasant, unpleasant, pleasant, or very pleasant

CH = {noHarm, causeHarmL, causeHarmH, willcauseHarmL, willcauseHarmH}

Please note: “OutcomeProbability is the measure of consequences (pleasant/unpleasant) of the event with respect to the goal.   Desirability is associated with each event as a measure of how desirable the event is in pursuit of the goal. Expectedness is the likelihood of that event to occur according to the agent’s world view or memory.”  (Jain et al 2015)

     To comprise a rule, a vector will be assigned to each state ready for appraisal. The vector input consists of the following:

     LHS  --- <” Event_ID”, “Time_stamp”, “Current_state_of emotion”,  Evar  >

Degrees or levels of emotions       (VeryHigh, High, Medium, Low, Neutral) The letter following the degree is first letter of the emotion.

Happy = {VHighH, HighH, MediumH, LowH, Neutral}

Sad = {VHighS, HighS, MediumS, LowS, Neutral}

Anger = {VHighA, HighA, MediumA, LowA, Neutral}

Fear = {VHighF, HighF, MediumF, LowF, Neutral}

Reproach = {VHighR, HighR, MediumR, LowR, Neutral}

View intelligent agent (“child”) and emotional relation to agent “God”.  An assumption to note:   “child” agent is of age 18 and above.

Note: A “child” agent can take role of “parent” agent as well.

 Event_ID = name of intelligent agent experiencing the event

  Variable t= constant (time)

 

Rule 1: if Desirability(Child, t) is high

               and Expectedness(Child, t) is medium  

               and Suddeness(Child, t) is low and

              and OutcomeProbability(Child, t) is pleasant

              and CauseHarm(Child, t) is noHarm

              then  RHS  -> Happy is VHighH (elicitation of emotion)

Rule 2: if (Fear is HighF) AND

             if Desirability(Child, t) is high

               and Expectedness(Child, t) is medium  

               and Suddeness(Child, t) is low and

              and OutcomeProbability(Child, t) is unpleasant

              and CauseHarm(Child, t) is noHarm

              then  RHS  -> Grateful is HighG (elicitation of emotion)

Rule 3: if (Fear is HighF) AND

             if Desirability(Child, t) is high

               and Expectedness(Child, t) is medium  

               and Suddeness(Child, t) is low and

              and OutcomeProbability(Child, t) is pleasant

              and CauseHarm(Child, t) is noHarm

              then  RHS  -> fate (amor-fati)  is HighF (elicitation of emotion)

Rule 4: if Desirability(Parent, t) is high

               and Expectedness(Parent, t) is medium  

               and Suddeness(Parent, t) is low and

              and OutcomeProbability(Parent, t) is pleasant

              and CauseHarm(Parent, t) is noHarm

              then  RHS  -> Gratitude is VHighG (elicitation of emotion)

 Rule 5: if Desirability(Parent /Child, t) is high

               and Expectedness(Parent/Child, t) is high  

               and Suddeness(Parent/Child, t) is high/medium

              and OutcomeProbability(Parent/Child, t) is unpleasant

              and CauseHarm(Parent/Child, t) is noHarm

              then  RHS  -> Anger is VHighA (elicitation of emotion)

  Rule 6: if Desirability(Parent /Child, t) is high

               and Expectedness(Parent/Child, t) is medium/low  

               and Suddeness(Parent/Child, t) is low

              and OutcomeProbability(Parent/Child, t) is unpleasant

              and CauseHarm(Parent/Child, t) is noHarm

              then  RHS  -> Reproach is HighR (elicitation of emotion)

* Since, the manifestation of atheist (feature)  is unknown,  an agent can inherit this trait any time via parent or the environment. Furthermore, the states of which an agent is appraising can change, thus manifesting a different personality. Viewing situation of “atheism” vs. “non-atheism”, for example, I appraise the world according to my own GSP and my parent’s GSP which we both possess personality of “non-atheism”. If state of the environment elicits emotions of anger or reproach, I may be compelled to change my disposition to “atheism”.  I will remain in that state indefinitely till another positive event occurs and I elicit a positive emotion resulting in a change in new disposition, back to “non-atheism”. In essence, this feature (atheism) is a relative variable and dependent on state transitions, resembling “finite state machine”.

   The intensity of the variables elicited (reproach or anger) changes the agent’s disposition from initially having belief in God to not believing in God, thus manifesting the trait (feature) of “atheism”.
